# Style DNA: Anthropic

> **Disclaimer:** This describes stylistic and motion *patterns* commonly observed in Anthropic's public launch content (e.g. claude.ai launch pages), for creative-direction reference only. Never reproduce Anthropic's actual logo, wordmark, exact brand colors, typeface, or copyrighted material. Adapt the pattern to your own brand — see `.claude/skills/brand-motion-system/references/reference-company-styles.md`.

## Color & type mood

- Warm, paper-like neutrals rather than cold corporate gray or pure white — a soft off-white/cream base reads as approachable rather than clinical.
- A single warm accent (terracotta/clay-adjacent tones are typical of this register) used sparingly, almost never as a full-background color.
- Typography: humanist serif or serif-adjacent for headline claims (unusual among tech companies, contributes to the "considered" feel), clean sans for body/UI text.
- Generous whitespace — content breathes rather than fills the frame edge-to-edge.

## Motion signature

- Slow, deliberate pacing. Very high hold ratio (70%+ hold to motion) — this register trusts silence and stillness more than most tech launch content.
- Cross-fades, never hard cuts, between major beats.
- Easing: smootherstep-style, no bounce, no overshoot — motion arrives and settles, it doesn't announce itself.
- Typographic reveals (text drawing in or fading in a word at a time) are a signature move, used at a measured pace — never rapid-fire.
- Very few simultaneous moving elements — usually exactly one thing moving at a time.

## Sound signature

- Understated to silent. If music is present, it's ambient and recedes rather than drives the pacing. No aggressive UI-click SFX.

## Typical narrative arc

Quiet, confident statement of intent → a small number of restrained product/feature reveals, each given room to breathe → a calm close, rarely a hard-sell CTA. Maps to the "Config/Community Keynote Arc" or a very restrained "Problem→Proof→Transformation" from `.claude/skills/creative-director/references/narrative-structures.md`, with the energy curve flattened rather than climbing sharply.

## Applied storyboard skeleton (generic product)

| Scene | Time | Purpose | Motion | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-8s | Warm, quiet opening statement | Single line of type fades/draws in, long hold | No logo yet |
| 2 | 8-20s | First reveal | One UI element or claim enters, slow cross-fade in | High hold ratio |
| 3 | 20-32s | Second reveal, slightly more specific | Same restrained motion register, no new visual language introduced | Consistency over novelty |
| 4 | 32-40s | Quiet close | Wordmark draw-in, single closing line | No aggressive CTA button |

## Best-fit audience

Enterprise buyers who value restraint and credibility over hype, and startups/dev-tools wanting to signal maturity and trustworthiness rather than scrappy energy. A poor fit for anything that needs to grab attention in a noisy, fast-scroll feed in under 2 seconds — this register rewards a captive, patient viewer.
