# Style DNA: Samsung (Unpacked-style)

> **Disclaimer:** This describes stylistic and motion *patterns* commonly observed in Samsung's public Unpacked-style launch events, for creative-direction reference only. Never reproduce Samsung's actual logo, real product photography, or copyrighted material. Adapt the pattern to your own brand — see `.claude/skills/brand-motion-system/references/reference-company-styles.md`.

## Color & type mood

- Glossy, high-production gradient backdrops — bolder and more saturated than the Apple/Anthropic registers, often multi-color gradients rather than a single accent.
- Multiple hero products/devices frequently share the frame (a phone, a watch, earbuds) rather than one object alone — the visual language is built for a multi-device ecosystem story.
- Type: clean geometric sans, used more densely than Apple's register — feature call-outs and spec labels appear more frequently and more simultaneously.

## Motion signature

- Fast-paced feature-stacking: multiple capabilities are shown in quick succession rather than one long dwell per feature — energy comes from volume and pace, not from singular restraint.
- Synchronized multi-device choreography: devices rotate/reveal in coordinated motion (e.g. a phone and a watch rotating in sync) — a distinctive technique when a genuine device ecosystem exists to show.
- Easing: confident and snappy (`outCubic`/`outBack`-leaning), shorter holds than Apple or Anthropic — this register assumes a more engaged, faster-consuming audience.
- Frequent cross-fades and swipe-transitions between feature segments, keeping momentum high throughout.

## Sound signature

- Energetic, produced music bed running through most of the piece, with musical hits timed to feature reveals; occasional UI-style chime on a feature-callout landing.

## Typical narrative arc

A compressed Feature→Benefit→Proof repeated rapidly across many features (more features, less time each, than the Feature-Benefit-Proof default) — see `.claude/skills/creative-director/references/narrative-structures.md`. Works well for an ecosystem/portfolio launch rather than a single hero object.

## Applied storyboard skeleton (generic multi-product/ecosystem launch)

| Scene | Time | Purpose | Motion | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-6s | Energetic cold open | Gradient backdrop, quick logo-adjacent motion | Fast pace established immediately |
| 2 | 6-16s | Feature stack 1 (device A) | Feature callouts stack in quickly | 2-3 features in this window |
| 3 | 16-26s | Feature stack 2 (device B) | Synchronized multi-device reveal | Devices move in coordinated motion |
| 4 | 26-34s | Ecosystem moment | All devices shown together | The "why they work together" beat |
| 5 | 34-40s | Close | Fast confident CTA card | Music resolves here |

## Best-fit audience

Consumer and enterprise hardware launches with a genuine multi-device or multi-feature portfolio to show, where energy and breadth matter more than singular restraint. A poor fit for a single-feature story (too much unused choreography) or a slow, considered enterprise review context.
