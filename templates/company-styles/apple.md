# Style DNA: Apple

> **Disclaimer:** This describes stylistic and motion *patterns* commonly observed in Apple's public keynote and product-reveal content, for creative-direction reference only. Never reproduce Apple's actual logo, exact product photography, typeface, or copyrighted material. Adapt the pattern to your own brand — see `.claude/skills/brand-motion-system/references/reference-company-styles.md`.

## Color & type mood

- Deep, near-black backdrops for hero product moments — darkness used to make a single product the only thing in the frame.
- Minimal on-screen color beyond the product itself; color comes from the product/material, not from graphic decoration.
- Type: clean, geometric-humanist sans, used extremely sparingly — often a single word or short phrase per beat, never paragraphs.
- Materials-forward: the "color palette" is often literally the product's real materials (metal, glass) rather than a graphic palette.

## Motion signature

- Cinematic camera language: slow macro push-ins, orbital moves around a hero product, shallow-depth-of-field-style focus pulls (simulated via blur/scale in a code-driven context).
- Extremely restrained copy density paired with confident, unhurried pacing — one claim per beat, held long enough to land fully before the next.
- Easing: smooth, physically-plausible deceleration into every stop — nothing snaps or jitters.
- Cuts are used deliberately at major beat changes (new feature, new angle) rather than cross-fading everything — the cut itself carries weight because it's rare.

## Sound signature

- A single, minimal musical motif rather than a busy track — often just a few notes or a swell at the reveal moment. No UI click SFX; this register doesn't simulate interface interaction, it presents product as object.

## Typical narrative arc

Data-Story Arc or a compressed Problem→Proof, almost entirely non-verbal in its persuasion: show the object, let one spec or capability speak, move to the next. Maps closely to `.claude/skills/creative-director/references/narrative-structures.md` → Data-Story Arc for spec-heavy reveals.

## Applied storyboard skeleton (generic hardware/product reveal)

| Scene | Time | Purpose | Motion | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-6s | Object reveal in darkness | Slow push-in, single light source | No copy yet |
| 2 | 6-16s | Single hero capability | One spec/claim appears, held | Camera orbits slowly |
| 3 | 16-26s | Second capability | Cut (not cross-fade) to new angle | New claim, same restraint |
| 4 | 26-34s | Scale/comparison moment | The "Data-Story Arc" number reveal | Number appears exactly once at full weight |
| 5 | 34-40s | Close | Product held in final hero frame | Wordmark last, understated |

## Best-fit audience

Consumer hardware launches and enterprise hardware/deep-tech reveals where the product itself is genuinely the hero and can carry a scene with minimal copy. A poor fit for feature-dense SaaS demos (see Linear/Stripe instead) or content that needs to explain a workflow rather than present an object.
