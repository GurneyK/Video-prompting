# Motion Principles

Use motion to help the viewer understand the story.

## Principles

1. Motion explains, it does not decorate.
2. One beat should have one dominant movement.
3. Hold important frames long enough to read.
4. Stagger related elements by 80-150ms.
5. Avoid simultaneous motion of unrelated elements.
6. Use consistent easing inside a scene.
7. Make the mid-scene frame look like a designed poster.
8. Let the final frame land cleanly.

## Good Uses Of Motion

- Revealing steps in sequence
- Showing before/after transformation
- Following a cursor through a workflow
- Moving data from source to output
- Transitioning from problem to solution
- Focusing attention on a decision or CTA

## Weak Uses Of Motion

- Background movement that competes with text
- Random floating elements
- Too many easing styles
- Moving everything at once
- Decorative spins, bounces, and zooms with no story reason

## Prompt

```text
Review this storyboard for motion discipline. Identify where motion explains the idea, where it distracts, and where holds should be longer.
```
