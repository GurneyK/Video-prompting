# Style DNA: SpaceX

> **Disclaimer:** This describes stylistic and motion *patterns* commonly observed in SpaceX's public livestream and mission content, for creative-direction reference only. Never reproduce SpaceX's actual logo, real telemetry data, or copyrighted footage. Adapt the pattern to your own brand — see `.claude/skills/brand-motion-system/references/reference-company-styles.md`.

## Color & type mood

- Dark, near-black or deep-navy technical backdrops throughout — this register almost never goes to a light background.
- Monospace or tabular-figure numerics for any live data/telemetry-style overlay — the numeric typography is doing a lot of the "this is real and precise" work.
- Sparse accent color, typically a single cool tone (blue/cyan-adjacent) for active-state UI elements, against otherwise grayscale data chrome.
- Overlay UI (data readouts, status labels) styled like real mission-control instrumentation — grid-aligned, small caps or all-caps labels, thin hairline dividers.

## Motion signature

- Data overlays update continuously and precisely — numbers ticking, status labels flipping between states — this is a register built around *live-feeling* data motion, not decorative motion.
- Countdown-driven pacing: time-to-event is often the actual structuring device of the piece, not just a story beat.
- Sudden, sharp cuts on milestone moments (a stage separation, a landing) rather than a cross-fade — the cut itself signals "something just happened."
- Camera language: wide static or slow-drift shots punctuated by a sudden zoom/cut at the milestone — stillness during "nominal" phases, sharp motion at the event.

## Sound signature

- Often near-silent except for real (or simulated) mission-audio-style communication and a low ambient tension bed; SFX, if used, favors low, mechanical, or radio-static-adjacent textures over musical chimes. Structural, not decorative.

## Typical narrative arc

Data-Story Arc, but time-anchored: context for why this milestone matters → countdown/build-up → the event itself, given maximum visual weight → immediate confirmation/data proof it worked → what this unlocks next. See `.claude/skills/creative-director/references/narrative-structures.md` → Data-Story Arc.

## Applied storyboard skeleton (generic technical/infra milestone reveal)

| Scene | Time | Purpose | Motion | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-8s | Context: why this milestone matters | Dark backdrop, telemetry-style overlay establishes stakes | Monospace numerics |
| 2 | 8-20s | Build-up / countdown | Ticking data, tension holds | Minimal cuts, mostly static holds |
| 3 | 20-24s | The milestone itself | Sharp cut, maximum visual weight | Single loudest visual/audio moment of the piece |
| 4 | 24-32s | Confirmation | Data overlay confirms success plainly | No celebration graphics — the data is the proof |
| 5 | 32-38s | What this unlocks | One forward-looking claim | Understated close |

## Best-fit audience

Deep-tech, infrastructure, hardware, and performance-benchmark reveals where credibility comes from precision and real data rather than emotional appeal — works for both enterprise infra announcements and deep-tech startups. A poor fit for consumer-emotional brand content (see Airbnb/Nike instead) or anything that needs warmth over precision.
