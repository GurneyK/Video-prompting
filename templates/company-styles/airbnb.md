# Style DNA: Airbnb

> **Disclaimer:** This describes stylistic and motion *patterns* commonly observed in Airbnb's public brand and product content, for creative-direction reference only. Never reproduce Airbnb's actual logo, real host/guest footage, or copyrighted material. Adapt the pattern to your own brand — see `.claude/skills/brand-motion-system/references/reference-company-styles.md`.

## Color & type mood

- Warm, naturalistic palette — soft coral/pink-adjacent accent against warm neutrals, evoking daylight and real places rather than a studio backdrop.
- Type: rounded, friendly sans, used at a comfortable, readable scale — never as aggressively bold or kinetic as Nike's register.
- Real-feeling environments (homes, neighborhoods, landscapes) are the visual backdrop far more than studio product shots — this register is documentary-adjacent even when product UI appears.

## Motion signature

- Gentle, human-paced motion — slower than Linear/Samsung, warmer than Apple's clinical restraint. Soft cross-fades, natural camera drift rather than mechanical push-ins.
- Product/UI is woven in late and lightly — this register leads with people/place and lets the product feel like a natural part of the story rather than the opening subject.
- Map and location motion (a pin dropping, a route drawing, a location zooming in) is a recurring, distinctive move — used to ground the story in a real place.
- Easing: soft, natural deceleration (`smootherstep`) — nothing sharp or mechanical.

## Sound signature

- Warm, often acoustic or organic-feeling music bed; ambient/environmental sound (if using real-feeling footage-style scenes) more than synthetic UI SFX.

## Typical narrative arc

Hero's Journey (compressed), documentary-flavored: a person/place is established first, a need or moment arrives, the product appears as a natural part of resolving it, close on the human outcome rather than the product. See `.claude/skills/creative-director/references/narrative-structures.md`.

## Applied storyboard skeleton (generic marketplace/platform product)

| Scene | Time | Purpose | Motion | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-8s | Establish a person/place, not the product | Gentle camera drift, warm palette | No UI yet |
| 2 | 8-16s | The need/moment arrives | Soft cross-fade to the specific situation | Still human-first |
| 3 | 16-26s | Product woven in naturally | Map/location motion, light UI touch | Product feels incidental, not the hero yet |
| 4 | 26-32s | Resolution | Human outcome shown, product recedes again | The feeling, not the feature, is the payoff |
| 5 | 32-36s | Close | Warm, quiet mark | No hard-sell CTA |

## Best-fit audience

Consumer brand and marketplace/platform stories where trust and human warmth matter more than a feature list — travel, home, community, and similar platforms. A poor fit for a technical dev-tool demo or an enterprise spec-driven reveal where this register's indirection would read as unfocused.
