# Style DNA: Figma (Config-style)

> **Disclaimer:** This describes stylistic and motion *patterns* commonly observed in Figma's public community-conference and product-launch content, for creative-direction reference only. Never reproduce Figma's actual logo, exact brand colors, typeface, or copyrighted material. Adapt the pattern to your own brand — see `.claude/skills/brand-motion-system/references/reference-company-styles.md`.

## Color & type mood

- Bright, multi-color accent system rather than a single restrained accent — this register embraces color as personality, closer to Samsung's boldness but friendlier and less glossy.
- Type: approachable, slightly rounded sans, used with confidence at a range of scales — headline claims can be large and playful without losing legibility.
- Community/collaboration motifs (multiple cursors, avatars, colorful overlapping shapes) are a recurring visual signature — the palette often reads as "many people, many colors, one canvas."

## Motion signature

- High energy but human, not mechanical — livelier than Linear's clinical speed, warmer than Samsung's glossy pace.
- Live-cursor/collaboration motifs animate playfully: multiple cursors moving, colliding, or handing off from one to another — a distinctive move for any collaboration-forward product.
- Easing: `outBack`-leaning for playful landing emphasis, used more freely than in restrained registers — bounce and overshoot are acceptable here in a way they aren't for Anthropic/Stripe.
- Reveals often stack quickly in a "and one more thing" rhythm (see Config/Community Keynote Arc) — energy climbs across a series of smaller reveals rather than one long build.

## Sound signature

- Upbeat, often synth-forward music bed; playful UI-click or "pop" SFX on collaborative moments (a cursor click, an element being added) fit this register well — more sound-forward than Stripe/Linear, less cinematic than Nike.

## Typical narrative arc

Config/Community Keynote Arc: warm, energetic cold open (community, not product) → a string of climbing reveals → a community/ecosystem close rather than a hard CTA. See `.claude/skills/creative-director/references/narrative-structures.md`.

## Applied storyboard skeleton (generic collaborative/community product launch)

| Scene | Time | Purpose | Motion | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-6s | Warm, energetic cold open | Multiple cursors/avatars animate playfully | Community energy, not product yet |
| 2 | 6-14s | Reveal 1 (small) | Quick, `outBack`-style landing | Sets a playful tone |
| 3 | 14-22s | Reveal 2 (bigger) | Energy climbs, faster stack | Building momentum |
| 4 | 22-30s | Reveal 3 (headline) | Biggest visual/audio moment | The "and one more thing" beat |
| 5 | 30-36s | Close | Community/ecosystem framing, not a hard sell | "Come build with us" tone |

## Best-fit audience

Startup and dev/design-tool products with a genuine community or collaboration angle, and internal "what shipped this quarter" content that wants energy without enterprise-restraint or Nike-level intensity. A poor fit for a single-hero-object hardware reveal or a restrained enterprise compliance-review context.
