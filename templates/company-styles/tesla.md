# Style DNA: Tesla

> **Disclaimer:** This describes stylistic and motion *patterns* commonly observed in Tesla's public product-unveiling content, for creative-direction reference only. Never reproduce Tesla's actual logo, real product photography, or copyrighted material. Adapt the pattern to your own brand — see `.claude/skills/brand-motion-system/references/reference-company-styles.md`.

## Color & type mood

- Single dramatic light source on a dark stage — the product is lit like a hero object, everything else recedes to near-black.
- Minimal color beyond the product's own material and a single accent (often red or a saturated brand color) used only for a callout or CTA, never as a background wash.
- Type: bold, condensed sans for spec callouts (numbers given real visual weight — large, confident, often the single biggest element on screen at that moment).
- Spec callouts favor big numerals with small supporting unit labels — the number itself is the graphic.

## Motion signature

- Dramatic reveal motion: a slow build (light rising, silhouette resolving) followed by a confident, fast reveal — the contrast between the slow build and the sudden clarity is the technique.
- Spec-callout typography animates in with snap and confidence — a fast, slightly punchy easing (`outBack`-adjacent) rather than a soft fade, appropriate for the "big number lands" moment specifically.
- Low overall scene count, high visual weight per scene — this register does more with fewer, bigger beats rather than many small ones.
- Camera language: slow orbits and reveals for the product itself, contrasted with fast, confident cuts to spec-callout cards.

## Sound signature

- A single rising musical swell timed to the reveal moment; minimal SFX beyond one weighted "thump" or hit at the reveal itself. Not click/UI-forward — this is a hardware-object register, not an interface register.

## Typical narrative arc

Data-Story Arc with a single dominant hero reveal rather than multiple: context/anticipation build → the reveal (given the single most visual weight in the piece) → spec callouts as proof → a confident close. See `.claude/skills/creative-director/references/narrative-structures.md` → Data-Story Arc.

## Applied storyboard skeleton (generic hardware/product unveiling)

| Scene | Time | Purpose | Motion | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-10s | Build/anticipation | Silhouette resolves slowly out of darkness | Single light source |
| 2 | 10-14s | The reveal | Fast, confident cut to full clarity | Musical swell peaks here |
| 3 | 14-22s | Spec callout 1 | Big numeral animates in with snap | `outBack`-style landing |
| 4 | 22-28s | Spec callout 2 | Same register, second stat | Consistency, not novelty |
| 5 | 28-34s | Close | Product held, single confident closing line | Minimal copy |

## Best-fit audience

Consumer hardware and deep-tech product unveilings where a single hero object and its specs are the whole story. A poor fit for multi-feature SaaS demos or anything without a genuine single hero object to light dramatically.
